Albert Einstein (1879 - 1955)

Timeline

1879

Albert Einstein is born on March 14 in Ulm, Germany

1884

Einstein's family moves to Munich, Germany

1896

He renounces his German citizenship and attends the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich

1905

"Annus Mirabilis" - Einstein publishes four groundbreaking papers on the photoelectric effect, Brownian motion, special relativity, and the equivalence of mass and energy (E=mcÂ²)

1907

Einstein publishes the idea that light can be thought of as particles, which would later be known as photons

1915

Einstein completes the theory of general relativity, explaining gravitation as the warping of spacetime by matter

1917

His prediction of gravitational lensing during a solar eclipse is confirmed, providing evidence for general relativity

1919

The Royal Society of London and the Royal Astronomical Society verify Einstein's predictions during a solar eclipse, making him famous worldwide

1921

Einstein is awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his work on the photoelectric effect

1933

Einstein leaves Germany due to the rise of the Nazi regime and moves to the United States

1939

He signs a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt, warning of the potential development of atomic weapons by Nazi Germany, which ultimately contributes to the Manhattan Project

1945

World War II ends, and Einstein becomes an advocate for nuclear disarmament

1952

Israel offers Einstein the presidency, but he declines

1955

Albert Einstein passes away on April 18 in Princeton, New Jersey, USA
